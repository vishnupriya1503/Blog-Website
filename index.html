The Eternal Elegance of the Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, situated in Agra, India, is a magnificent white marble mausoleum commissioned by Emperor Shah Jahan in 1632 to honor his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth.

Completed in 1653, this architectural masterpiece is celebrated for its perfect symmetry, intricate marble inlay with semi-precious stones, and the majestic central dome flanked by four minarets. Combining elements of Islamic, Persian, and Indian architectural styles, the Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. Its timeless beauty and the poignant love story it embodies draw millions of visitors from around the globe each year.
